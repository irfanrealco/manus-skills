# Pattern Recognition Framework

This framework guides deep pattern analysis when working with raw ideas, stream-of-consciousness content, or unstructured thinking.

## Types of Patterns to Identify

### Behavioral Patterns
How the person actually operates, regardless of what they say:
- **Pressure-creation mechanisms** - Do they work best under deadlines? Do they pre-commit to create urgency?
- **Decision-making triggers** - What causes them to take action vs. procrastinate?
- **Energy patterns** - When do they create best? What drains them?
- **Relationship patterns** - How do they interact with others? What causes trust or withdrawal?

### Cognitive Patterns
How they think and process information:
- **Learning style** - Do they learn from pressure, instruction, observation, or experimentation?
- **Systems thinking** - Do they see connections, build frameworks, or think in layers?
- **Truth-seeking behavior** - Do they challenge assumptions? Accept authority? Test reality?
- **Meta-cognition** - Are they aware of their own thinking patterns?

### Emotional Patterns
Underlying emotional drivers and wounds:
- **Core wounds** - What past experiences shape current behavior?
- **Protection mechanisms** - How do they protect themselves from pain?
- **Avoidance patterns** - What do they consistently avoid or suppress?
- **Motivation sources** - What truly drives them (beyond stated goals)?

### Strategic Patterns
How they approach goals and vision:
- **Vision architecture** - Do they think in sequences, layers, or simultaneous tracks?
- **Resource allocation** - Where do they invest time, energy, money?
- **Risk tolerance** - Do they embrace uncertainty or seek safety?
- **Long-term vs. short-term** - Do they sacrifice now for later, or vice versa?

## Pattern Analysis Questions

When analyzing raw ideas, ask:

1. **What's the pattern underneath the pattern?** - Look beyond surface behavior to root causes
2. **What are they building toward?** - Identify the meta-goal behind individual ideas
3. **What are they avoiding?** - Notice what's conspicuously absent from their thinking
4. **What contradictions exist?** - Where do stated beliefs conflict with behavior?
5. **What's the deeper "why"?** - Why does this matter to them at a fundamental level?

## Extracting Gems of Wisdom

Gems are small, concentrated insights that reveal deep truths. Look for:

- **Compressed wisdom** - A single sentence that reveals how they operate
- **Paradoxes** - Contradictions that point to deeper complexity
- **Metaphors they use** - How they describe their experience reveals their worldview
- **Repeated themes** - What shows up again and again across different contexts
- **Unspoken assumptions** - What they take for granted that others might not

## Pattern Documentation Template

For each major pattern identified:

**Pattern Name:** [Concise, memorable name]

**What this reveals:** [The underlying truth about how they operate]

**The pattern:** [Description of the behavior/thinking pattern]

**The wisdom:** [What this teaches about effective operation]

**The application:** [How to use this insight practically]

**The danger:** [What could go wrong if this pattern is overused or misapplied]

## Meta-Patterns

Look for patterns of patterns:

- **How do their patterns connect?** - Do they form a coherent system?
- **What's the core operating principle?** - Is there one principle that explains multiple patterns?
- **What's the origin story?** - How did these patterns develop?
- **What's the evolution trajectory?** - Where are these patterns leading them?
