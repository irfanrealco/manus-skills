# Pricing Strategy: The Pricing Triangle

## Purpose

Set prices that are above average (signaling quality), accessible (not excluding your buyer), and defensible (you can explain why it costs what it costs).

## The Pricing Triangle

```
        CEILING
       (perceived value)
          /\
         /  \
        /    \
       /  SET \
      /  PRICE \
     /    HERE  \
    /____________\
  FLOOR          ANCHOR
(cost to deliver) (competitor price)
```

**Floor** = What does it cost you in time and tools to deliver this once?
Calculate: (hours × your hourly rate) + (tool costs) + (support overhead)

**Ceiling** = What is the buyer's perceived value?
Ask: "What would it cost the buyer to get this result another way?" (hiring a developer, buying a course, spending 20 hours themselves)

**Anchor** = What do competitors charge for the closest equivalent?
Research: Fiverr, Gumroad, Upwork, Reddit posts, community pricing discussions.

**Set price** = Slightly above the anchor, well below the ceiling, comfortably above the floor.

## Market Research Method

1. Search Fiverr for `[capability] setup` — note the range of prices
2. Search Gumroad for `[capability]` — note what digital products sell for
3. Search Upwork for `[capability] freelancer` — note hourly rates and project budgets
4. Search Reddit for `"how much does [capability] cost"` — note community expectations
5. Calculate the average of all prices found
6. Set your price at 115–130% of that average

## Objection Handling Scripts

**"That's too expensive."**
> "I understand. What would it cost you to figure this out yourself? Most people spend 8–15 hours on this and still end up with a broken setup. At your hourly rate, that's [calculate]. This is cheaper and it actually works."

**"I can find someone cheaper on Fiverr."**
> "You can. The difference is that Fiverr installs are generic — they don't security-harden, they don't configure for your specific workflow, and there's no support after. This is a concierge service, not a commodity install."

**"Can I get a discount?"**
> "I don't discount, but I do offer a payment plan if timing is the issue. What's your situation?"

## Pricing Rules

- State the price plainly. Never hide it behind "contact for pricing."
- Never offer a quote when a price is listed. The price IS the quote.
- Never use subscription pricing unless the product genuinely delivers recurring value that the buyer cannot get any other way.
- Never price below your floor. Underpricing signals low quality and attracts bad clients.
- Review pricing after every 5 clients. If everyone says yes immediately, raise the price.
